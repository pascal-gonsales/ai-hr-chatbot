## One Sentence

TeamChat AI is an AI-assisted employee lifecycle system for restaurant groups, taking an employee from access request and day-one onboarding through training, daily HR support, incident/request handling, growth, offboarding, and exit interview, while keeping managers in control and records auditable.

## Product Thesis

Restaurant owners and managers do not need a generic HR chatbot. They need an operational layer that:

- answers repetitive employee questions from approved internal knowledge,
- turns messy employee messages into structured cases,
- guides employees through onboarding and training without manager hand-holding,
- preserves a clear paper trail for sensitive issues,
- reduces interruptions during service,
- supports multilingual teams,
- helps managers see what needs action today.

The AI is not the authority. The authority is approved knowledge, source systems, manager decisions, and auditable records.

## Target Users

### Employee

Typical needs:

- understand what to do on day 1,
- know policies without asking a manager during rush,
- check tips, hours, schedule, and requests,
- report issues safely,
- learn role-specific procedures,
- confirm completion of training,
- ask in their strongest language.

Product promise:

- "I can ask without feeling stupid, get clear next steps, and have a record."

### Manager / Owner

Typical needs:

- onboard staff faster,
- reduce repetitive HR questions,
- know which requests require action,
- keep employee communication documented,
- identify training gaps,
- handle incidents and departures consistently,
- maintain control over policies and decisions.

Product promise:

- "The system organizes employee work and flags what I must decide."

### Admin / HR Operator

Typical needs:

- maintain policy and training knowledge,
- assign training by role/location,
- monitor cases and urgent issues,
- approve or correct AI-generated drafts,
- produce records for later review.

Product promise:

- "The system gives me clean queues, not scattered messages."

## Product Principles

1. **AI is interface, not authority.** It explains, routes, summarizes, and drafts. It does not decide employment outcomes.
2. **Every operational answer has a source.** Policies, SOPs, training steps, tips, hours, and requests must trace to an approved record or system.
3. **One employee, one lifecycle.** The same product owns onboarding, training, daily support, incidents, growth, offboarding, and exit interview.
4. **Managers stay in control.** Anything that affects employment status, pay, discipline, safety, harassment, leave approval, or termination requires human review.
5. **Mobile-first and service-aware.** Restaurant employees use this before/after shifts, on breaks, and under time pressure.
6. **Multilingual by design.** The employee can speak in their language; official records and manager-facing output use the business language configured by the restaurant.
7. **Structured cases beat loose chat.** Important conversations become cases with owner, status, urgency, source, due date, and audit trail.
8. **Training is operational, not academic.** Lessons must map to real tasks, stations, policies, and manager sign-offs.
9. **Do not invent policy.** If knowledge is missing, the AI says it is missing and creates a manager/admin task.
10. **Privacy is a feature.** Employees only see their own data; managers see only what their role permits.

## What The Product Must Never Become

- A system that gives legal advice, payroll-law advice, immigration advice, medical advice, or mental-health diagnosis.
- A system that makes disciplinary, termination, promotion, pay, leave, or accommodation decisions automatically.
- A generic chatbot with no source trail.
- A place where sensitive employee complaints disappear into chat history without a case.
- A tool that exposes one employee's schedule, pay, tips, complaint, or personal data to another employee.
- A training library that cannot prove who completed what, when, and under which version.

## Long-Term North Star

For every employee, the system can show:

- who they are,
- their role and location,
- what they were taught,
- which SOP version they acknowledged,
- what requests or incidents occurred,
- what managers decided,
- what remains open,
- what happened at exit.

For every manager, the system can show:

- who needs onboarding today,
- who is blocked,
- what cases are urgent,
- what training is overdue,
- what policy gaps employees keep asking about,
- what decisions require manager action.

## Success Metrics

Early metrics:

- percent of employees with completed day-one onboarding,
- average time to first completed training module,
- number of employee questions answered from sourced KB,
- number of requests converted into structured cases,
- manager response time on urgent cases,
- reduction in repeated policy questions,
- percent of AI answers with source references,
- percent of cases closed with documented outcome.

Later metrics:

- training completion by role/location,
- incident response time,
- onboarding retention after 30/60/90 days,
- unresolved case age,
- policy gap frequency,
- manager workload saved,
- employee satisfaction after onboarding and exit.
